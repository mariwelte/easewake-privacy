## What we don't do

- We don't directly collect any personal data.
- We don't operate a server. The app never sends your data anywhere.
- We don't use third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.
- We don't ask for an account, email, or password.
- We don't process personal data within the meaning of Art. 4(1) GDPR through the app itself.

## What stays on your device

Your alarms, nudge configurations, sound choices, and onboarding state are stored locally on your iPhone using Apple's standard SwiftData and UserDefaults frameworks. This data never leaves your device. If you delete the app, this data is permanently removed.

## What Apple sees

EaseWake uses AlarmKit, Apple's system alarm framework. Apple — not us — handles the actual alarm scheduling, fire delivery, and Lock-Screen presentation. Apple's privacy policy applies to that part. AlarmKit runs locally on your device; we never send your alarm data to Apple.

## Apple App Analytics (optional, opt-in)

If you have enabled "Share with App Developers" in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements, Apple shares aggregated, anonymized crash and usage statistics with us. This data is technically and legally anonymized by Apple before we receive it. We cannot identify individual users from this data. You can disable this setting in iOS at any time.

Legal basis (where applicable): Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR — legitimate interest in identifying app crashes and improving stability. Since the data is anonymized at source by Apple, no personal data within the meaning of GDPR is processed by us.

## TestFlight beta participation

If you join EaseWake through Apple's TestFlight beta program, Apple shares additional data with us per Apple's TestFlight terms: tester email address (provided by you to Apple), crash logs, feedback submissions, app usage time, and device type. This data is processed solely for the purpose of beta testing and is deleted after the beta phase. Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — performance of the testing agreement you enter with us through TestFlight participation.

Production users from the App Store release are not affected.

- Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR)
- Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)
- Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)
- Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
- Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
- Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR)
