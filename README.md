# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 14 May 2026**

EaseWake is a local alarm app. We respect your privacy by not collecting it.

## What we don't do

- We don't collect personal data.
- We don't have a server. The app never sends your data anywhere.
- We don't use third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.
- We don't ask for an account, email, or password.

## What stays on your device

Your alarms, nudge configurations, sound choices, and onboarding state are stored locally on your iPhone using Apple's standard SwiftData and UserDefaults. They never leave your device. If you delete the app, this data is gone.

## What Apple sees

EaseWake uses AlarmKit, Apple's system alarm framework. Apple — not us — handles the actual alarm scheduling, fire delivery, and Lock-Screen presentation. Apple's privacy policy applies to that part. We never send your alarm data to Apple either; AlarmKit runs locally on your device.

## Apple App Analytics

If you've enabled "Share with App Developers" in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements, Apple may share aggregated, anonymized crash and usage statistics with us. This is opt-in on your side, controlled entirely through Apple's settings. We have no way to identify individual users from this data. You can turn it off any time.

## TestFlight beta participation

If you joined EaseWake through TestFlight, Apple shares additional beta-feedback data with us per Apple's TestFlight terms. Production users (App Store releases) are not affected.

## Your rights

Because we don't collect data, there's nothing to request, correct, or delete on our side. To remove all EaseWake data, delete the app from your iPhone.
